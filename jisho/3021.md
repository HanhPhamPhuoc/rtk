### On-Yomi: ヘキ &mdash; Kun-Yomi: たま

## Heisig V6:

The <b>holed gem</b> to which this kanji refers is a flat, circular object of about a foot in length, made of stone or glass and having a large hole in the middle. It's elements: <i>ketchup</i> . . . <i>ball</i>.

## Koohii stories:

1) [<a href="http://kanji.koohii.com/profile/thecite">thecite</a>] 8-12-2009(6): <strong>rest against</strong>: When there’s a strong wind, it’s your responsibility to rest against something to prevent being blown away.

2) [<a href="http://kanji.koohii.com/profile/megaboyx">megaboyx</a>] 5-1-2010(2): The <em>hatted samurai</em> knows to take <em>responsibility</em> when he <strong>leans against</strong> something because if a <em>gust of wind</em> should come from underneath, you could fall over and break something. Variation: 靠 (revelation+jail cell) <strong> lean against (HYOU HEI motareru yoru)</strong>.

3) [<a href="http://kanji.koohii.com/profile/endote">endote</a>] 7-9-2012(): It&#039;s your responsibility not to lean on something that will be blown away in the wind.
